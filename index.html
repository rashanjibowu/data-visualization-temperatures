Historical Deviations from Normal Temperature By Meteorological Season

The visualization below shows annual deviations from "normal" temperature, where "normal" temperature is defined as the average temperature from 1951 to 1980. The data is shown in reverse chronological order in order to see current relative temperatures first.

This visualization explores the potential vertical timelines. Each year is plotted on the y-axis and temperature deviations are plotted on the x-axis, split by meteorological season.

As shown below, our planet is getting warmer (across all seasons) and has been doing so at a remarkable pace since 1980. The rate of change between 1880 and 1950, while still positive, is much slower than the current pace.
